Another example - in CSS

Took this novice a full day of research and experimenting back and forth, to figure out how to get a laser beam shoot around the email button when hovered.

The challenge was the four nth-children's transform and transition properties; having their values run smoothly in relation to each other. The beam's four lines "travelling" perimetrically, as either X or Y around the anchor tag, obviously depend on correct percentage and time set for a smooth, steady flow.

As you can see, I started with color #7ABFF5 all around, but to simplify the trial and error process, each nth-child got a different color instead. That instantly helped spot how say child(3) behaved according to its sibling on the opposite side - child(1) - and whether its values needed adjusting. The colors ended up staying, each representing the degree they cover around the button: pink 90 deg, orange 180 deg, green 270 deg and white 360 deg.

I like printing codes like this on paper when I'm done and writing notes around them. It helps with the learning process a lot, but... I'm ready to shift gear.
